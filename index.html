I’m a Pathology Resident and Academic Clinical Lecturer based in Edinburgh, UK. I hold a joint appointment with NHS Lothian and the University of Edinburgh, where I am based at the Centre for Regenerative Medicine (Institute for Regeneration and Repair). I am funded through the Edinburgh Clinical Academic Training (ECAT) programme under the Scottish Clinical Research Excellence Development Scheme (SCREDS).

My research focuses on somatic and cancer evolution, with a particular interest in how genomic instability — including aneuploidy and extrachromosomal DNA (ecDNA) — drives tumour initiation, progression, and recurrence. I am especially keen on integrating spatial multi-omics approaches with histopathology to better understand these processes in clinically relevant tissues.

I’m always interested in new collaborations and ideas at the intersection of genomic instability, spatial biology, and pathology. Please feel free to get in touch if you’d like to explore potential synergies.

Research

We are interested in tracking and targeting genomic instability in cancers by studying tumours predominantly in their native tissue contexts. Our work focuses on three main themes:

Mechanisms underlying genomic instability

Morphology-aware spatial multi-omics

Biomarkers and therapeutics
